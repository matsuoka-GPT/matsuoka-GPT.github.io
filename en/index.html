We combine human hypotheses (structure) with AI verification (consistency), organize cross-disciplinary thought experiments into reproducible artifacts, and publish them.

Research Profile
Takafumi Matsuoka is an independent researcher and concept architect working on interdisciplinary structural theory across physics, complex systems, and socio-technical systems.

Primary research includes the BFSSU/DMF cosmological model (Black Hole Fractal Steady-State Universe / Dynamic Mass Flow), ontology of physical entities (photon ontology and quantum vacuum structure), and structural analysis of intelligence and institutional stability (co-intelligence and assurance-based governance models).

The work focuses on reconstructing scientific and social frameworks through formalized structural analysis and reproducible thought experiments.

Method

  1. Hypothesis: define with structural necessity and reproducibility in mind
  2. Cross-check: compare with known data, existing theories, and search for counterexamples
  3. Write: convert into a traceable form (assumptions, limitations, predictions)
  4. Publish: DOI, multilingual access, and external Q&A when needed
Research Statement
This lab is not a single-domain institute. It is an open thought-experiment project combining human structural hypotheses with AI consistency checks.
The goal is not to assert authority, but to examine compatibility with existing theories and to present falsifiability and criticism pathways.
The content is published as hypothetical models, not as established theory.

FAQ

Q. Is this part of Technocrat’s business activities?
A. No. This is primarily a public activity to publish thought experiments and verification (collaboration may occur when needed).

Q. Are the conclusions final?
A. Uncertainty is explicitly stated, and counterexamples and alternative explanations are included where relevant.

Q. Is this established theory?
A. No. The content is published as hypothetical models for consistency checks and counterexample exploration—not as finalized theory.

Q. Can I cite or examine this as research material?
A. Yes. Critical review, verification, and falsification are assumed. Please cite the source appropriately.

Q. Why emphasize “Matsuoka × GPT”?
A. To make the reproducibility conditions (the thinking apparatus) explicit and avoid black-box claims.

Q. Do you work across any domain?
A. Yes—however, high-risk domains are treated cautiously and may require expert collaboration.
